# Topic 1.3 - The Roles of Business Analyst

## 1. The Interpreter Role of Business Analyst

Business analysts requires decisions and skills in interpretation, judgment, and communication - more than just numbers, algorithms, or datasets:

- Business analysts sit at the intersection of data and decision-making.

    - They are not just technicians or model-builders.

    - They are **interpreters** to make data meaningful to the people who need to act on it.

- Business analyst needs a blend of skills: business understanding, analytical fluency, and communication.

    - This skillset is not limited to advanced degrees - undergraduate analysts with proper training can create significant value from the start.

    - The course is designed to build this foundational skillset for effective business analysts.

> Citation:
> "MBAs who have good business intuition but also speak the language of statisticians are rock stars."
> -- Susan Athey, Professor of Economics at Stanford

Business analysts are modern storytellers:

- Business analysts use data to tell clear and truthful stories that support better decision-making.

- Business analyst’s role is to build a bridge between the data and the decision, for example:

    - Why sales are falling

    - How investor sentiment is improving

    - What might happen if the firm raises prices or changes strategy



## 2. The Core Skills of Business Analysts

Business analysts need a blend of skills:

- Business understanding:

    - Business analysts must understand the context

    - For example: What is the decision? What matters to stakeholders? What constraints shape the options?

- Data analysis:

    - Business analysts need to clean, explore, and model data using the right tools

    - This includes descriptive, predictive and causal tools to support strategy

- Communication and storytelling:

    - Business analysts must craft narratives that are statistically sound

    - These narratives must also be understandable, relevant, and actionable

These skills grow through practice:

- Working on real problems

- Engaging with real data

- Reflecting on how analysis shapes action



## 3. Collaboration Role of Business Analysts

Business analysts have to work with:

-   Data engineers - build the infrastructure and pipelines that make analysis possible.

-   Business stakeholders - define the problems and act on the results.

-   Other analysts - bring complementary expertise and perspectives.

Business analysts are glue in the collaboration - keeps the analytical process connected to business goals, ethical standards, and practical decisions.
